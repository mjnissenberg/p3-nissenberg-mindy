America Reads

America Reads is one of the preeminent federal community service programs in the country.Our tutors play a critical role in elementary schools and community centers throughout Gainesville. They partner with teachers to support reading instruction. They work one-on-one with children who are struggling to learn. They run small-group tutoring sessions. They inspire a love of books and reading. They are literacy superheroes! Are you a UF student who is eligible for Federal Work Study? Want to make a difference in the life of a child learning to read?

James Patterson Literacy Challenge

Funded with the generous support of the world's best-selling author, the work of the James Patterson Literacy Challenge centers around teacher development. Our intensive,school-based professional development efforts have focused on deepening teachers’ knowledge and enhancing their classroom application of evidence-based instructional methods. In addition to our work with teachers, we have also broadened our scope to include supporting the development of district leaders and school administrators in order to help school districts build capacity and create systems in which our teacher development work can thrive.

Learn more about the James Patterson Literacy Challenge  

Summer Adventures in Literacy (SAIL)

The UFLI Summer Adventures in Literacy (SAIL) program takes place each summer for approximately 20 days. SAIL programming targets foundational reading skills, including decoding and oral reading fluency. Instruction during SAIL is delivered by UF special education graduate students under the supervision of UF faculty and instructors. Eligibility for SAIL participation is determined through literacy screening assessment data.

Learn more about SAIL  

Graduate Certificate in Dyslexia

The Dyslexia Certificate is a fully online program that is designed for practicing educators and other related service professionals. Upon completion, participants will be able to (a) conduct and interpret effective literacy assessments and (b) design and implement evidence-based literacy intervention for students with dyslexia. Requirements for admission include an earned Bachelor’s Degree, preferably in Education or a related area of study, and there is an expectation that potential students have some prior knowledge and experience in teaching reading.

UFLI Tutoring and Instructional Models

UFLI works to improve literacy outcomes for students who struggle to learn to read and write. They have created several tutoring and instructional models that target the needs of struggling readers as well as provide effective Tier 1 instruction for the entire class. The UFLI Intensive Intervention is designed for one-on-one work with students with dyslexia. UFLI Basic is a one-on-one tutoring model for working with emerging readers. UFLI Small-Group was designed to address the same literacy difficulties as the UFLI Tutoring for Beginning Readers program, but with groups of three to five students, rather than one-on-one.

Learn more about UFLI Instructional Models
