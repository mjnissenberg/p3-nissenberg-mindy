The University of Florida Literacy Institute (UFLI - pronounced "you fly") is an ongoing effort by UF faculty and students to improve literacy outcomes for struggling studentsby addressing two key areas: reader developmentand teacher development.

Reader Development programs focus on direct assessment, instruction, and intervention services to students.

Teacher Development programs prepare preservice teachers and practicing educators to use evidence-based instructional practices to serve struggling readers. These programs include graduate coursework, school-based supports, and online professional learning experiences to reach teachers worldwide.

One of UFLIâ€™s newest creations is the Virtual Teaching Resource Hub, a one-stop source for literacy activities and lesson templates that can be used in online reading instruction, as well as professional learning resources. To date, the Hub has had over 2.5 million visitors from all around the world.

Mission and Vision

The University of Florida promotes effective teaching and enhanced learrning for children struggling with reading, through transformative teacher development and reader development programs.
